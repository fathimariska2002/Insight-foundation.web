Building for the Long Term

The Financial Backbone of a Permanent Mission

Rather than relying solely on annual fundraising cycles, the Endowment and Waqf Fund is structured to grow over time and generate a sustainable income stream — permanently underwriting scholarships for Zakat‑eligible students in alignment with the Foundation's equity‑driven mission. This enduring financial model ensures that future generations of learners, regardless of circumstance, will continue to access education, skills, and opportunity through the Insight Foundation.

A scholarship funded from an endowment is a scholarship that does not rise and fall with the uncertainties of annual fundraising. Every contribution to the Insight Foundation Endowment and Waqf Fund is invested prudently and permanently.

Only the income generated — never the principal — is used to sustain scholarships year after year. This model provides a clear answer to the question every donor eventually asks:

"What happens to my gift after this year?"

Through the Endowment and Waqf Fund, your contribution continues working indefinitely, supporting future generations of learners long after the initial donation is made.

Governed by the Investment Oversight Committee

Expert Oversight at Every Level

The Fund is supervised by the Investment Oversight Committee (IOC) — a multi-disciplinary body maintaining formal compliance with Shariah principles and Sri Lankan financial regulations.

Chair

Board Representative

Provides governance continuity and ensures the IOC's decisions remain aligned with the Foundation's constitutional mandate and long-term mission.

Committee Member

Islamic Finance Professional

Oversees the Shariah-Compliant Investment Policy Statement (IPS) and ensures all investment vehicles conform to AAOIFI standards and halal finance principles.

Committee Member

Legal & Regulatory Advisor

Ensures full compliance with Sri Lanka's Companies Act, Inland Revenue regulations, and Central Bank investment guidelines at all times.

Committee Member

Risk Management Specialist

Maintains liquidity reserves to meet annual scholarship obligations and monitors portfolio risk to ensure the principal is never eroded.

Committee Member

Qualified Shariah Advisor

Commissions and reviews the independent annual Shariah audit, certifying continued adherence to recognised Islamic jurisprudence in all Fund activities.

Non-Voting Representative

Scholarship Committee

Provides the IOC with visibility into scholarship demand, disbursement schedules, and student outcomes — linking investment performance to real-world impact.

The IOC maintains a formal Shariah-Compliant Investment Policy Statement (IPS), conducts quarterly reviews of investment performance and compliance, and commissions an independent annual Shariah audit to ensure continued adherence to AAOIFI standards.
Governance & Compliance Framework

Accountability You Can Rely On

Quarterly investment performance and Shariah compliance review by the full IOC

Annual independent Shariah audit commissioned and reviewed against AAOIFI standards

Strict segregation of Zakat-designated funds from general Waqf capital at all times

Liquidity reserves maintained to meet annual scholarship obligations without eroding long-term capital

Full compliance with Sri Lanka's Companies Act, Inland Revenue regulations, Central Bank investment guidelines, and TVEC financial governance expectations

Transparency to Donors

See Exactly How Your Contribution Works

The IOC provides a comprehensive transparency framework ensuring every donor has full visibility into how contributions are managed and the impact they create — year after year.

Annual Donor Impact Reports

Comprehensive yearly reports covering investment performance, scholarship disbursements, and measurable outcomes — delivered directly to every Fund contributor.

Zakat-Specific Reporting

Donors who designate contributions as Zakat receive dedicated reporting confirming exclusive allocation to verified Zakat-eligible recipients, fully separated from general Waqf capital.

Public Scholarship Outcome Disclosure

Enrolment, graduation, and employment indicators are publicly disclosed, ensuring donors can track the real-world impact of the Fund on learner outcomes.

Zakat Compliance Certificates

Available on request, confirming that your contribution has been deployed strictly in accordance with Shariah principles and Miskath Research Institute's guidance.

Zakat Compliance Certificate

Available to all Endowment & Waqf Fund donors who designate their contribution as Zakat. Confirms your gift was:

  • Allocated exclusively to a verified Zakat-eligible student
  • Held in a fully segregated Zakat account
  • Deployed in accordance with Shariah-compliant guidelines
  • Reviewed under the annual independent Shariah audit
  • Endorsed by the Miskath Research Institute, Sri Lanka
Miskath Research Institute endorsed — Sri Lanka's leading Shariah research body formally endorses the Foundation's Zakat stewardship processes.
